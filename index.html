our
process

01
planning & conceptualizing

Architecture is a collaborative process, not a solitary one. There is always a client and an interpreter for that client's demands. The client-architect relationship is critical, and the formation of a professional and trusted relationship between the two is the foundation of any successful project. Planning & Design conceptualization is the process of coming up with ideas for the best solution to a design problem. These concepts should be derived from the product concept and the specified definitions of the design problem. This is marks the initial phase of discussion between the architect and the client where ideas will be exchanged for

02
design & development

The schematic drawings and elevations are evaluated, changed, and expanded during design development to include all of the features and standards required for construction. The project's components are scrutinized down to the slightest detail.

03
CONSTRUCTION DRAWING & DOCUMENTATION

All of the details, dimensions, and notes required to communicate the overall design intent to the builder are included in these drawings and specifications. Construction Documents are frequently the most time-consuming step, which might surprise clients because the design appears to be complete after Design Development with the customer.

04
COORDINATION & SUPERVISION

When a client approaches an architect, a meeting is set up to discuss the needs and requirements of the project. It is probable that multiple meetings will be required until both parties are pleased with the conceptual design. However, designing a complete structure is a colossal task for a single person; architects interact with civil and structural engineers to discuss technical issues and the structural integrity of a building.

05
FURNITURE, FABRIC & LIGHTING SELECTIONS

If a preliminary presentation is unanimously approved, the designers can go on to the final design stages. If revisions are required, another meeting with revised presentations may be required. The following stages of the design may include a sequence of drawings created by professional draftsmen or by the interior designer himself, if he works as an independent contractor. Final drawings may consist of a few sheets or a significant number of drawings, depending on the type of task. Plans, elevations, details, sections, and specifications are the language of architectural and design offices, and they are prepared with meticulously drawn dimensions and comments for the numerous contractors who carry out the aforementioned projects.
